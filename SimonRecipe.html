Adobo
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 (3 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • ⅔ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
 Adobo is a classic Filipino dish known for its savory, tangy flavor and tender meat, slow-cooked in a rich, flavorful sauce.

Palabok
  • 12 ounces palabok noodles (cornstarch sticks)
  • 2 to 3 quarts water (for soaking the noodles)
  • 1 Knorr Shrimp Cube
  • 8 ounces pork shoulder, sliced into small pieces
  • ½ cup tinapa flakes (smoked fish flakes)
  • 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup annatto water
  • 3 ½ cups water
  • 3 ½ tablespoons fish sauce
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 10 to 12 pieces shrimp, deveined, shell and head removed
  • 1 cup Napa cabbage, sliced into small squares
  • 3 boiled eggs, sliced into quarters
  • ½ cup crushed chicharrón (pork rinds)
  • ½ cup chopped scallions
 Palabok, or Pancit Palabok, is a type of Filipino noodle dish made with cornstarch sticks, also known as palabok noodles. It features an orange sauce made from minced pork, tinapa flakes (smoked fish flakes), and annatto water.

Lugaw
  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 4 to 5 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • ¼ cup rousong (pork floss)
 Lugaw is a type of rice porridge from the Philippines, flavored with aromatics and topped with toasted garlic, scallions, and a boiled egg.
