Adobo
  1. 2 lbs pork belly
  2. 2 tablespoons garlics
  3. 5 dried bay leaves
  4. 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  5. 4 tablespoons vinegar
  6. 1/2 cup soy sauce
  7. 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  8. 2 cups water
  9. Salt to taste
  • Combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and garlic then marinade for at least 1 hour
  • Heat the pot and put-in the marinated pork belly then cook for a few minutes
  • Pour remaining marinade including garlic.
  • Add water, whole pepper corn, and dried bay leaves then bring to a boil. Simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour
  • Put-in the vinegar and simmer for 12 to 15 minutes
  • Add salt to taste
  • Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
Sisig
  1. 1 lb. of pig ears
  2. 1 lb. of pig snout
  3. 1 lb. of pork belly
  4. 1 quart of water (for boiling)
  5. 2 onions, minced
  6. 4 thumbs of ginger, crushed
  7. 2 teaspoons of salt
  8. 3 bay leaves
  1. ½ cup of Lady’s Choice Mayonnaise
  2. 2 tablespoons of sukang iloko
  3. ¼ teaspoon of ground black pepper
  4. 1 teaspoon of salt
  5. 1 teaspoon of sugar
  6. ¼ cup of liver spread
  7. 2 limes (for added freshness)
  8. 1 tablespoon of Knorr Liquid Seasoning
  • Combine pig ears, snout, pork belly, ginger, bay leave Boil for 1 hour. Remove from the pot and drain the water.
  • Heat-up your grill. Start grilling the pig parts for around 5 minutes per side.
    Remove from the grill afterwards and let it cool down.
  • Make the dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients. Mix well.
  • Chop the grilled pig parts into small pieces.
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped pork with onion and the dressing mixture.
    Toss until all the ingredients are well blended.
  • Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with your favorite drink. Share and enjoy
Palabok
  1. 12 ounces palabok noodles cornstarch sticks
  2. 2 to 3 quarts water for soaking the noodles
  1. 1 Knorr Shrimp Cube
  2. 8 ounces pork shoulder sliced into small pieces
  3. ½ cup tinapa flakes smoked fish flakes
  4. 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  5. ½ cup annatto water
  6. 3 ½ cups water
  7. 3 ½ tablespoons fish sauce
  8. ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  9. 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  1. 10 to 12 pieces shrimp deveined, shell and head removed
  2. 1 cup Napa cabbage sliced into small squares
  3. 3 boiled eggs sliced into quarters
  4. ½ cup crushed chicharon pork rinds
  5. ½ cup chopped scallions
  • Pour 2 to 3 quarts water in a large mixing bowl or pot.
    Soak the palabok noodles in water for 30 minutes
  • Remove the soaked noodles from the bowl and set aside.
  • Meanwhile, pour the water in a large cooking pot. Let boil.
  • Put the noodles into the pot.
    Cook for 10 to 15 minutes or until soft.
    Remove noodles fromeing exposed in air,
    the pot and drain the excess water.
    Place the noodles in a large bowl and cover it (this will prevent it from b which can eventually dry the noodles). Set aside.
  • Add shrimp on the same pot with boiling water.
    Cook for 1 ½ minutes or until it float0 Remove from the pot and set aside.
  • Put Napa cabbage into the pot. Boil for 1 minute. Remove and set aside.
  • Prepare the sauce by pouring oil in a clean cooking pot.
  • Once the oil gets hot, add the pork. Sauté until light brown
  • Pour fish sauce into the pot along with the annatto water. Stir.
  • Pour 3 ½ cups water into the pot and let it boil.
    Cover the pot and continue to cook in medium heat for 15 to 18 minutes
    or until the pork gets tender.
  • Remove the cover and Knorr Shrimp Cube and tinapa flakes.
    Continue to cook for 5 minutes.
  • Combine flour with ½ cup water. Stir until texture becomes smooth.
    Pour the mixture into the pot. Stir.
    Continue to cook until desired thick texture is achieved.
  • Add the ground black pepper. Stir.
  • Assemble the palabok by placing noodles in a plate.
    Pour and sliced boiled eggs. the sauce over the noodles and then top with Napa cabbage, shrimp,
    Sprinkle crushed chicharon and chopped scallions on top.
Menudo
  1. 2 lbs. pork
  2. 1/4 lb. pig liver
  3. 1 cup potatoes, diced
  4. 1 piece carrot, cubed
  5. 1/2 cup soy sauce
  6. 1/2 piece lemon
  7. 1 piece onion, chopped
  8. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  9. 1 teaspoon sugar
  10. 3/4 cup tomato sauce
  11. 1 cup water
  12. 4 pieces hotdogs, sliced diagonally
  13. 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  14. 2 to 3 pieces dried bay leaves
  15. Salt and pepper to taste
  • Combine pork, soysauce, and lemon in a bowl. Marinate for at least 1 hour.
  • Heat oil in a pan
  • Saute garlic and onion.
  • Add the marinated pork. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes.
  • Pour in tomato sauce and water and then add the bay leaves.
    Let boil and simmer for 30 minutes to an hour depending on the
    toughness of the pork.
  • Add-in the liver and hot dogs.Cook for 5 minutes.
  • Put-in potatoes, carrots, sugar,salt, and pepper.
    Stir and cook for 8 to 12 minutes.
